\chapter{Syllables}

All syllables must have exactly one vowel;
syllablic consonants are not allowed.
In native items, \phonem{d, d͡ʒ, g, ɣ, l, ŋ, ɾ, w, z} do not appear word\-/%
initially, and \phonem{b, d͡ʒ, d, g, ɣ} do not appear in codas.
Additionally, complex onsets are not allowed at all, while complex codas are
subject to restriction;
only \phonem{jt, nt͡ʃ, nt, ɾk, ɾq, ɾs, ɾt} are allowed as complex codas.
Thus, the maximal syllable structure in native items is (C)V(C)(C)~(\vref
{ex:native-syllables}).

\pex\label{ex:native-syllables}
  \a (C)V:\\
    \vtop{\halign{
      \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil\cr
      ɛ.ki&two\cr
      bu&this\cr
    }}
  \a (C)VC:\\
    \vtop{\halign{
      \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil\cr
      ɑltɯ&six\cr
      jɯɾ&song\cr
    }}
  \a (C)VCC:\\
    \vtop{\halign{
      \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil\cr
      ɑnt&oath\cr
      qɯɾq&forty\cr
    }}
\xe

Loans may contain syllables of different types from those describe above, such
as those with complex onsets, other types of complex codas, or less restricted
distributions of consonants~(\vref{ex:loan-syllables}).

\ex\label{ex:loan-syllables}%
  \vtop{\halign{
    \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil&\:\copiedfrom~\foreign{#}\hfil&\:(#)\hfil%
    \cr
    hɑd͡ʒ&pilgrimage to Mecca&ḥajj&Arabic\cr
    dɔst&friend&dōst&Persian\cr
    gɾɑ.nɑ.tɑ&grenade&granata&Russian\cr
  }}
\xe

Polysyllabic words are always syllabified to have V.CV or C.CV splits;
vowel hiatuses are not allowed.
