\chapter{Syllables}

All syllabes must have exactly one vowel;
syllabic consonants are not allowed.
Vowels must be separated by at least one intervening consonant;
vowel hiatuses are not allowed.

In native items, \phonem{d, d͡ʒ, g, ɣ, l, ŋ, ɾ, w, z} are not allowed word\-/%
initially,
while \phone{b, d͡ʒ, d, g, ɣ} are not allowed in syllable codas.
Additionally, complex onsets are not allowed,
while only \phonem{jt, nt͡ʃ, nt, ɾk, ɾq, ɾs, ɾt} are allowed as complex codas.
Thus, the maximal syllable structure in native items is (C)V(C)(C),
with examples of (C)V in (\vref{ex:cv-syllable}), (C)VC in (\vref
{ex:cvc-syllable}), and (C)VCC in (\vref{ex:cvcc-syllable}).

\pex
  \a\label{ex:cv-syllable}%
    \vtop{\halign{\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil\cr
      \underline{ɛ}.ki&two\cr
      bu&this\cr
    }}
  \a\label{ex:cvc-syllable}%
    \vtop{\halign{\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil\cr
      \underline{ɑl}.tɯ&six\cr
      jɯɾ&song\cr
    }}
  \a\label{ex:cvcc-syllable}%
    \vtop{\halign{\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil\cr
      ɑnt&oath\cr
      qɯɾq&forty\cr
    }}
\xe

In loans, syllables that do not conform to these restrictions may be allowed.
This includes syllables with complex onsets, other types of complex codas, and
generally less restricted distributions of consonants.

\ex
  \vtop{\halign{\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil\cr
    hɑd͡ʒ&pilgrimage\cr
    dɔst&friend\cr
    pɾɔ.gɾɑm.mɑ&computer program\cr
  }}
\xe
