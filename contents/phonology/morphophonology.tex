\chapter{Morphophonology}

\section{Last\-/vowel deletion}

Certain disyllabic stems of the form (C)VCVC drop their second vowel if that
vowel is high and a suffix with an initial consonant is added~(\vref
{ex:deletion-yes}).
Whether or not this process applies to a particular stem is unpredictable;
each case must be memorised individually~(\vref{ex:deletion-no}).

\pex
  \a\label{ex:deletion-yes}%
    \vtop{\halign{
      \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil&\:+~\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\inlinegls{#}%
      \hfil&\:\derivedas~\phonem{#}\hfil\cr
      ɑwuz&mouth&\=/(I)m&\Poss.\Fsg&ɑwz\=/um\cr
      buɾun&nose&\=/(I)ŋ&\Poss.\Ssg&buɾn\=/uŋ\cr
      fikiɾ&intellect&\=/(s)I&\Poss.\Third&fikɾ\=/i\cr
    }}
  \a\label{ex:deletion-no}%
    \vtop{\halign{
      \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil&\:+~\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\inlinegls{#}%
      \hfil&\:\derivedas~\phonem{#}\hfil\cr
      ɛʃik&door&\=/(I)mIz&\Poss.\Fpl&ɛʃig\=/imiz\cr
      sɛkiz&seven&\=/(I)ncI&\Ord&sɛkiz\=/inci\cr
    }}
\xe

\section{Final\-/consonant lenition}

In native items, stem-final \phonem{p, k, q} become \phonem{b, g, ɣ}
respectively if a suffix with an initial vowel is added~(\vref
{ex:lenition-yes}).
This does not happen if the final consonant in question is part of a complex
coda~(\vref{ex:lenition-no}).

\pex
  \a\label{ex:lenition-yes}%
    \vtop{\halign{
      \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil&\:+~\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\inlinegls{#}%
      \hfil&\:\derivedas~\phonem{#}\hfil\cr

      qulɑq&ear&\=/(s)I&\Poss.\Third&qulɑɣ\=/ɯ\cr
    }}
  \a\label{ex:lenition-no}%

\section{Vowel harmony}

Interturkic has a system of vowel harmony where the quality of a given vowel
determines the quality of the next vowel within the word.
This occurs both within stems and when suffixes are joined to a stem.

\subsection{Within stems}

With the exception of loans and compounds, words must consist entirely of front
vowels or of back vowels.
Furthermore, rounded vowels must not follow unrounded vowels.

\subsection{Within suffixes}

Vowel harmony encompasses two mechanisms working in tandem:
backness harmony and rounding harmony.
Backness harmony requires a given vowel's backness to match that of the
preceding vowel.
Likewise, rounding harmony requires a given vowel's roundedness to match that of
the preceding vowel.
Derived from these two mechanisms are two types of vowel harmony (table~\vref
{tbl:vowel-harmony}).

\begin{table}
  \centering
  \caption{Vowel harmony}
  \label{tbl:vowel-harmony}
  \begin{tabular}{lllll}
    \toprule
    \multirow[t]{2}{*}{Type}&\multicolumn{2}{l}{Front}
    &\multicolumn{2}{l}{Back}\\
    \cmidrule(lr){2-3}\cmidrule(lr){4-5}
    &unrounded&rounded&unrounded&rounded\\
    \midrule
    A&\multicolumn{2}{l}{\phonem{ɛ}}&\multicolumn{2}{l}{\phonem{ɑ}}\\
    I&\phonem{i}&\phonem{y}&\phonem{ɯ}&\phonem{u}\\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

Suffixes joined to stems\---of any type\---always harmonise with the last vowel
of such stems, whether that vowel is part of the root itself or part of another
suffix.

A\=/type harmony alternates between \phonem{ɛ} in front\-/vowel environments
and \phonem{ɑ} in back\-/vowel environments~(\vref{ex:a-type-harmony}).
I\=/type harmony alternates between \phonem{i} in front unrounded environments,
\phonem{y} in front rounded environments, \phonem{ɯ} in back unrounded
environments, and \phonem{u} in back rounded environments~(\vref
{ex:i-type-harmony}).

\pex
  \a\label{ex:a-type-harmony}
    \vtop{\halign{%
      \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil&\:+~\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\inlinegls{#}%
      \hfil&\:\derivedas~\phonem{#}\hfil\cr
      sœz&word&\=/lAɾ&\Pl&sœz\-/lɛɾ\cr
      buzɑw&calf&\=/lAɾ&\Pl&buzɑw\-/lɑɾ\cr
    }}
  \a\label{ex:i-type-harmony}%
    \vtop{\halign{
      \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil&\:+~\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\inlinegls{#}%
      \hfil&\:\derivedas~\phonem{#}\hfil\cr
      ɛʃɛk&donkey&\=/nI&\Acc&ɛʃɛk\-/ni\cr
      kymyʃ&silver&\=/nI&\Acc&kymyʃ\-/ny\cr
      ɑɣɑt͡ʃ&tree&\=/nI&\Acc&ɑɣɑt͡ʃ\-/nɯ\cr
      jɔl&road, way&\=/nI&\Acc&jɔl\-/nu\cr
    }}
\xe

Within the context of morphophonology, diphthongs formed with \phonem{w} are
treated as if they were rounded monophthongs~(\vref{ex:rounded-diphthongs}).

\ex\label{ex:rounded-diphthongs}%
  \vtop{\halign{
    \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil&\:+~\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\inlinegls{#}\hfil&%
    \:\derivedas~\phonem{#}\hfil\cr
    biɾɛw&someone&\=/nI&\Acc&biɾɛw\-/ny\cr
    tɑw&mountain&\=/nI&\Acc&tɑw\-/nu\cr
  }}
\xe

\section{Consonant assimilation}

Certain consonants in suffixes undergo assimilation in accordance with their
surrounding environments.
Two factors govern this process:
the voicing of the immediately preceding consonant and the backness of the
preceding vowel.
This leads to three possible types of consonant assimilation (table~\vref
{tbl:consonant-assimilation}).

\begin{table}
  \centering
  \caption{Consonant assimilation}
  \label{tbl:consonant-assimilation}
  \begin{tabular}{lllll}
    \toprule
    \multirow[t]{2}{*}{Type}&\multicolumn{2}{l}{Unvoiced}
    &\multicolumn{2}{l}{Voiced}\\
    \cmidrule(lr){2-3}\cmidrule(lr){4-5}
    &unrounded&rounded&unrounded&rounded\\
    \midrule
    D&\multicolumn{2}{l}{\phonem{t}}&\multicolumn{2}{l}{\phonem{d}}\\
    G&\phonem{k}&\phonem{g}&\phonem{q}&\phonem{ɣ}\\
    K&\phonem{k}&\zero&\phonem{q}&\zero\\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

D\=/type assimilation depends on the first of the two factors, alternating
between \phonem{t} in unvoiced environments and \phonem{d} in voiced
environments~(\vref{ex:d-type-assimilation}).
K\=/type assimilation depends on the second factor, alternating between \phonem
{k} in front\-/vowel environments and \phonem{q} in back\-/vowel environments~(%
\vref{ex:k-type-assimilation}).
G\=/type assimilation depends on both factors, alternating between K\=/type
assimilation in unvoiced environments, \phonem{g} in front voiced environments,
and \phonem{ɣ} in back voiced environments~(\vref{ex:g-type-assimilation}).

\pex
  \a\label{ex:d-type-assimilation}%
    \vtop{\halign{
      \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil&\:+~\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\inlinegls{#}%
      \hfil&\:\derivedas~\phonem{#}\hfil\cr
      bulut&cloud&\=/DAn&\Abl&bulut\-/tɑn\cr
      biz&we&\=/DAn&\Abl&biz\-/dɛn\cr
    }}
  \a\label{ex:k-type-assimilation}%
    \vtop{\halign{
      \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil&\:+~\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\inlinegls{#}%
      \hfil&\:\derivedas~\phonem{#}\hfil\cr
      biɾ&one&\=/lIK&\Denom&biɾ\-/lik\cr
      dɔst&friend&\=/lIK&\Denom&dɔst\-/luq\cr
    }}
  \a\label{ex:g-type-assimilation}%
    \vtop{\halign{
      \phonem{#}\hfil&\:\transl{#}\hfil&\:+~\phonem{#}\hfil&\:\inlinegls{#}%
      \hfil&\:\derivedas~\phonem{#}\hfil\cr
      kɛt\=/&go&\=/GAn&\Ptcp.\Pst&kɛt\-/kɛn\cr
      jɑt\=/&lie&\=/GAn&\Ptcp.\Pst&jɑt\-/qɑn\cr
      kœɾ\=/&see&\=/GAn&\Ptcp.\Pst&kœɾ\-/gɛn\cr
      bɔl\=/&become&\=/GAn&\Ptcp.\Pst&bɔl\-/ɣɑn\cr
    }}
\xe

If there is no immediately preceding consonant, then the environment is treated
as voiced for the purposes of this process.
