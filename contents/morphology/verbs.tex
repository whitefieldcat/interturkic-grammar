\subsection{Person suffixes}

Finite verbs are marked with suffixes that agree with the grammatical subject in
person and number.
Table~\ref{tbl:person-suffixes} shows the three main sets of suffixes that are
used with different paradigms.

\begin{table}
  \centering
  \caption{Person suffixes}
  \label{tbl:person-suffixes}
  \begin{tabular}{lllllll}
    \toprule
    &\Fsg&\Ssg&\Tsg&\Fpl&\Spl&\Tpl\\
    \midrule
    Pronominal&\latntxt{\=/mAn}&\latntxt{\=/sAn}&\latntxt{\=/\zero}&\latntxt{%
    \=/mIz}&\latntxt{\=/sIz}&\latntxt{\=/[lAr]}\\
    Converbial&\latntxt{\=/mAn}&\latntxt{\=/sAn}&\latntxt{\=/DI}&\latntxt{%
    \=/mIz}&\latntxt{\=/sIz}&\latntxt{\=/DI[lAr]}\\
    Possessive&\latntxt{\=/m}&\latntxt{\=/ñ}&\latntxt{\=/\zero}&\latntxt{\=/K}&
    \latntxt{\=/ñIz}&\latntxt{\=/[lAr]}\\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}
